“ON CLOTHES”

KHALIL GIBRAN

FROM THE PROPHET

And the weaver said,

“Speak to us of Clothes.”

And he answered:

Your clothes conceal much of your beauty,
yet they hide not the unbeautiful.

And though you seek in garments the freedom of privacy
you may find in them a harness and a chain.

Would that you could
meet the sun and the wind

with more of your skin
and less of your raiment,

For the breath of life
is in the sunlight

and the hand of life
is in the wind.

Some of you say,

“It is the north wind who has
woven the clothes to wear.”

But shame
was his loom,

and the softening of the
sinews was his thread.

And when his work was done,

he laughed in the forest.

Forget not that modesty is for a shield
against the eye of the unclean.

And when the unclean shall be no more,
what were modesty but a fetter and
a fouling of the mind?

And forget not

that the earth delights

to feel your bare feet

and the winds long
